Culture of Bangladesh

Bangladesh has a rich and diverse culture influenced by its history, geography, and people.

Festivals such as Pohela Boishakh and Ekushey February are celebrated nationwide.

Traditional music, dance, and art play a significant role in Bangladeshi society.

Bengali cuisine is famous for its use of spices, rice, and fish.
  • Language: Bengali (Bangla)
  • Major Festivals: Pohela Boishakh, Eid, Durga Puja
  • Traditional Dress: Sari, Panjabi, Lungi
  • Famous Art Forms: Nakshi Kantha, Rickshaw Art
  • National Symbols: Royal Bengal Tiger, Water Lily

The culture of Bangladesh is a rich tapestry of traditions, literature, music, dance, and art. The Bengali language and literature have a long history, with Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore being a prominent figure. Traditional music includes Baul, Nazrul Geeti, and Rabindra Sangeet. Festivals, both religious and secular, are celebrated with enthusiasm. The cuisine is known for its flavorful curries, rice dishes, and sweets like roshogolla and sandesh.
